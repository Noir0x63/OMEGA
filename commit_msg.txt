[SECURITY] Hardened PFS & ECDSA Attestation Patch (VULN-01 & VULN-02)

Auditor: Eduardo "Noir0x63" Camarillo

This commit resolves two critical structural vulnerabilities in the ZTAP v3.1 E2EE implementation:

1. VULN-01: Perfect Forward Secrecy (PFS) Mitigation
   - Converted the inert ECDH shared secret into an active cryptographic binding.
   - Replaced pure PBKDF2 with a two-stage PBKDF2 -> HKDF Key Derivation Function.
   - Enforced a hard failure (`SEC_FAULT_NO_ECDH`) if the ephemeral ECDH secret is missing, eliminating silent degradations to zero-salt.
   - Added pre-worker initialization guards to prevent race conditions during ECDH handshakes.

2. VULN-02: Zero Trust Attestation Leak Remediation
   - Completely removed symmetric HMAC-based attestation which leaked the private key to the server.
   - Migrated the worker to generate a volatile, asymmetric ECDSA (P-256) key pair per session.
   - Enforced `extractable: false` on the private key, ensuring it never leaves the Web Worker memory.
   - Updated the server to import the client's SPKI public key and verify signatures using IEEE P1363 encoding, eliminating the server's capability to forge client identities.

Also includes comprehensive Red Team Audit Reports detailing the findings, failed initial implementations, and the definitive cryptographic resolutions.
